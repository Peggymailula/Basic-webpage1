Foreign Language:

Kiswahili

Kiswahili also known as Swahili is a language I would love to learn because it is indigenous to Africa.Many African countries lost their native languages due to colonization,however Swahili managed to survive thus making it that much more special.It's a national language in Kenya, Uganda and Tanzania, and an official language of the East African Community which comprises Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi and South Sudan.

Historical Places in Cape Town:

District Six Museum

  • It was established in 1867, one of six districts in Cape Town.
  • It was a vibrant centre with close links to the city and the port.
  • It is not only filled with personal memoirs and mementos, but also with poignant photographs.

The Castle of Good Hope

  • Built between 1666 and 1679 by the Dutch East India Company, it's the oldest existing colonial building in South Africa.
  • During Dutch colonial times, the five-point star fort became the Cape headquarter for the VOC.
  • It was built by soldiers, volunteers, slaves and Khoisan people.

City Hall

  • Nelson Mandela made his first free speech from a balcony at the Cape Town City Hall in 1990.
  • The tower houses a clock and a number of bells, modelled on the famous Big Ben.
  • It now plays host to a number of cultural and artistic events such as performances by the Cape Town Symphony Orchestra.
